2025 was the year I learned that building from scratch isn't about external validation ‚Äî it's about the intrinsic excitement of understanding complexity, building taste, and pursuing mastery. It was also a year of overwhelming but valuable travel, where I learned to appreciate the privilege of movement while recognizing when to pause.

This year brought significant growth in my career, deeper relationships with family and friends, and a lot of movement‚Äîboth geographically and mentally. Here's what stood out.

## Career: Building Taste and Hard things

The biggest shift this year was starting to build from scratch. I launched [smoldb](https://github.com/KShivendu/smoldb), a database project that's teaching me more about early design decisions than any code review ever could. Having full freedom to express my ideas is rapidly improving my programming taste.

At Qdrant, I worked on some of our most important features for our 2025 roadmap:

- **Read-write segregation** that will power multi-zone/region cluster sync
- **[Tiered multi-tenancy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=72Ux-Lgx93w)** ‚Äî one of our biggest milestones this year
- Major improvements to our distributed systems and storage layer: consensus, shards, replication, and the cluster manager service that I built.

Beyond distributed systems, I dove deep into ML (thanks Karpathy Sensei) and [reinforcement learning](https://github.com/kshivendu/grpo). The outcomes might not be visible yet, but the foundation has been laid.

The most unexpected career moment? Receiving 2 inbound offers from USA companies with O1 (Extraordinary talent) visa sponsorship. While I didn't pursue them due to personal reasons, it was a huge confidence boost ‚Äî a signal that I have the option to move to the USA if I ever wanted to. üöÄ

## Speaking and Sharing Knowledge

I gave talks at several places this year, including Microsoft Bangalore, PES University, and IIT Bhilai (my alma mater). Each of these opportunities was meaningful and rewarding in its own way.

Delivering my **first international conference talk at DjangoCon Chicago** was a truly unforgettable experience ‚Äî the opportunity to engage with a global audience was intimidating but it was smoother than I anticipated.

I also organized the first-ever IIT Bhilai alumni chapter meetups in Bangalore and Delhi and attended conferences like Fifth Elephant and Rust/Systems/Python meetups, where I made some acquaintances/friends.

## Travel: Overwhelming But Valuable

This was a year of frequent travel‚Äîoverwhelming at times, but I don't regret any of it. I'm grateful for the privilege to have visited multiple cities and countries:

- **United States**: Flew to Chicago for speaking at DjangoCon followed by outings and a road trip with college friends. Then I flew to San Francisco ‚Äî my dream city. I visited Stanford to meet my junior and a college friend, met past mentors and friends, attended meetups.
- **Europe**: Qdrant offsite in Mallorca Island, Spain, followed by Italy (Venice, Florence, Rome). Italy is now my favorite country in Europe.
- **India**: Multiple trips across the country ‚Äî family trips to North India (hometown and Delhi) and South India (Mysore, Ooty, Rameshwaram), ending the year with a beautiful Kerala trip (Kochi, Allepey, Trivandrum, Varkala).

The travel was intense and sometimes exhausting, but each trip brought something valuable: reconnecting with family, meeting mentors, experiencing new cultures, and building relationships.

## Learning and Growth üí°

Beyond technical skills, this year brought several important realizations:

- **Relationships that shape us**: This year's travel, meetups, and time with family and friends reminded me that relationships are investments that compound over time. The mentors who guided me, the friends I reconnected with, and the family moments I prioritized ‚Äî these connections have shaped where I am today, and I want to give back to them.

- **The joy of mastery**: Building smoldb in public has given me zero external gains so far, but that's not why I'm doing it. I'm intrinsically motivated by the excitement of understanding all this complexity. Building from scratch forces you to make foundational decisions that expose gaps in your understanding‚Äîyou can't hide behind existing abstractions. The pursuit of mastery itself is the reward, and it's accelerating my growth in ways that reading code or working within established systems never could.

- **Validation vs. pursuit**: Getting O1 visa offers was validating, but the bigger lesson was learning to separate external validation from internal direction. I'm already building things I'm excited about, and while certain opportunities might accelerate my timeline, I've learned that career optimization isn't the only optimization that matters.

- **Sharing accelerates learning**: Giving talks and writing forced me to articulate ideas clearly. The process of explaining concepts publicly‚Äîwhether in a conference talk or a blog post‚Äîrevealed what I actually understood versus what I only thought I understood.

- **Mental health as foundation**: Prioritizing mental well-being wasn't a nice-to-have‚Äîit became clear it's the foundation everything else builds on. This investment pays dividends in focus, creativity, and resilience.

## What I'd Do Differently

Looking back, I wish I had:

- Started building from scratch earlier ‚Äî projects like smoldb are improving my programming taste faster than anything else, and I should have begun this journey sooner
- Prioritized health and sleep from day one ‚Äî treating these as non-negotiables rather than "work in progress" would have improved my entire year, especially during the intense travel periods
- Spent more time on non-technical hobbies ‚Äî having interests outside of programming would have provided better balance and perspective in life, and likely made me more creative in my technical work

## What's Next ‚≠ê

2026 feels like it will be a year of transitions. I'll be turning 25, which feels like a big mental milestone ‚Äî a shift from early twenties to mid-twenties that brings different perspectives and priorities. I'm focusing on:

- **More ML**: I'm expecting to be more hands on with machine learning in 2026. The foundation I laid this year with ML and reinforcement learning should start showing more visible outcomes
- **Maintaining balance**: I want to be more intentional about spending time on relationships, travel, hobbies, and life outside of work ‚Äî not just because it's healthy, but because these experiences make the work itself more meaningful.
- **Pursuing mastery**: Continuing to build [smoldb](https://github.com/kshivendu/smoldb) (and other smol projects) and dive deeper into the complexity‚Äînot for external validation, but for the intrinsic satisfaction of understanding the universe/systems
